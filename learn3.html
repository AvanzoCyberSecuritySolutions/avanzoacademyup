Course Overview

In today's hyper-connected world, every click, search, and social media post leaves a trail and that trail can be exploited. This course empowers learners to understand how everyday digital actions can lead to vulnerabilities, and how to safeguard themselves using simple, practical cybersecurity strategies.

Through expert-led instruction, hands-on demos, real-life case studies, and interactive exercises, learners will explore how hackers think, how attacks evolve, and how to build habits that protect digital lives both personally and professionally.

Module 1: Understanding Cybersecurity and Common Threats

  • What is cybersecurity and why it matters to everyone
  • Real-life examples of malware, ransomware, phishing, and scams
  • Why attackers target individuals, not just organizations
  • The evolving threat landscape in a digital-first world

Module 2: How Hackers Think – The Attack Lifecycle

  • The hacker mindset and motivation
  • Reconnaissance to exploitation: the typical flow of a cyberattack
  • Real-world case walkthroughs of basic and advanced attacks
  • Why human behavior is the weakest link

Module 3: Passwords and Authentication – Your First Line of Defense

  • How passwords are cracked: brute force, dictionary attacks, and leaks
  • Creating strong, memorable passwords and passphrases
  • Password managers and multi-factor authentication
  • Common password mistakes and how to avoid them

Module 4: OSINT – Hacking Without Hacking

  • Introduction to Open-Source Intelligence (OSINT)
  • Data collection methods: usernames, metadata, Google Dorking
  • Ethical vs unethical data use
  • Hands-on tools like Sherlock, Dehashed, and ExifTool

Module 5: Social Engineering and Phishing Attacks

  • Manipulating human psychology to breach security
  • Types of social engineering: pretexting, baiting, tailgating
  • Anatomy of a phishing email or message
  • Spotting red flags and verifying authenticity

Module 6: Digital Hygiene and Privacy Protection

  • What is a digital footprint and how it’s exploited
  • Cleaning up your online presence
  • Social media privacy settings and best practices
  • Tools for blocking trackers, removing metadata, and checking breaches

Module 7: Cyber Safety in Daily Life

  • Safe browsing habits and mobile security
  • Using public Wi-Fi securely
  • Device-level protection: antivirus, updates, and firewalls
  • Backups and data recovery basics

Module 8: Responding to Threats and Building Cyber Resilience

  • What to do if your account is hacked or data is leaked
  • Reporting scams and protecting others
  • Building a personal or organizational cyber action plan
  • Resources for staying informed and empowered
